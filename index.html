TRANSIT RIDERS UNION CT!


REMEMBER WHEN THE BUSES WERE FARE FREE?

Governor Ned Lamont ended fare-free busses in April of 2023, resulting in a significant drop in ridership. Lamont and our legislators lack political willpower to deliver adequate equitable and sustainable services to Connecticut communities, despite the urgency of the climate crisis and a large transportation budget surplus for the state.

Public Transit Must Be Free And Accessible To All

Working people, tenants, and all residents of Connecticut deserve public transit that gets them to work, to school, to medical care, and which supports their lives. As transit riders, you have the power to organize with TRUCT and demand a public transit system that works for the people who use it. Join us using the links below and start talking to your fellow transit riders & workers, and other people on your commute. What does fare free transit mean to you? How can our transit rider union fight for transportation equity in Connecticut?
